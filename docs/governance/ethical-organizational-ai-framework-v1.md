# Ethical Organizational AI Framework v1

## Overview

TatakelolaKampus is designed as a reflective organizational intelligence system for higher education governance.

The platform is NOT intended to:
- monitor individuals,
- punish organizational actors,
- automate institutional authority,
- replace deliberative governance.

Its primary purpose is:
to support reflective, participatory, adaptive, and humane institutional learning.

---

# Core Ethical Principles

## 1. Human-Centered Governance

Human interpretation remains central.

AI outputs are:
- advisory,
- interpretive,
- reflective.

Final institutional decisions must remain:
human, deliberative, contextual, and accountable.

---

## 2. Non-Surveillance Principle

TatakelolaKampus is NOT a surveillance platform.

The system must not be used to:
- profile individuals,
- monitor personal political positions,
- punish dissent,
- identify emotional vulnerability,
- track personal behavior.

The system focuses on:
organizational patterns and institutional dynamics.

---

## 3. Participatory Legitimacy

Governance intelligence should strengthen:
- participation,
- transparency,
- collaborative dialogue,
- institutional trust.

The system must not reinforce:
- authoritarian governance,
- opaque decision-making,
- symbolic participation,
- bureaucratic domination.

---

## 4. Interpretive Humility

Organizational reality is complex.

AI-generated interpretations are:
- partial,
- probabilistic,
- contextual,
- revisable.

The platform must avoid:
- deterministic labeling,
- institutional overconfidence,
- simplistic classification.

---

## 5. Institutional Dignity

All institutional actors possess:
- dignity,
- agency,
- contextual complexity.

The system must avoid:
- stigmatization,
- dehumanization,
- reductionist categorization.

Organizational tensions should be interpreted:
constructively and ethically.

---

# Ethical Constraints

The system must NOT:
- rank human worth,
- produce punitive recommendations,
- automate sanctions,
- infer psychological diagnoses,
- manipulate organizational emotions.

The platform is:
a reflective governance companion,
not a behavioral control mechanism.

---

# Responsible Use Guidelines

TatakelolaKampus should be used for:
- governance reflection,
- institutional learning,
- organizational dialogue,
- adaptive coordination,
- resilience strengthening.

The system should NOT be used for:
- political targeting,
- institutional intimidation,
- disciplinary automation,
- personal reputation scoring.

---

# Transparency Principle

Institutions using TatakelolaKampus should:
- disclose AI-assisted governance analysis,
- explain analytical limitations,
- maintain interpretive accountability,
- preserve human review mechanisms.

Institutional actors should understand:
how organizational interpretations are generated.

---

# Data Ethics

Datasets should:
- minimize personal exposure,
- avoid unnecessary identifiers,
- prioritize organizational abstraction,
- preserve contextual integrity.

Anonymization and aggregation are strongly recommended.

---

# Governance Philosophy

TatakelolaKampus adopts:
a reflective-governance philosophy.

The platform assumes:
healthy institutions emerge from:
- dialogue,
- participation,
- adaptive learning,
- collective intelligence,
- ethical governance reflection.

---

# Ethical AI Position

TatakelolaKampus rejects:
- technocratic authoritarianism,
- automated governance domination,
- surveillance-based administration,
- reductionist organizational control.

The framework supports:
humanistic institutional intelligence.

---

# Long-Term Ethical Vision

The long-term vision of TatakelolaKampus is:

to develop ethical institutional AI systems that strengthen democratic, participatory, adaptive, and resilient higher education governance ecosystems.

The platform aims to become:
an ethical companion for institutional transformation,
not an instrument of domination.
