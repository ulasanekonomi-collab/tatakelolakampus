# Institutional Pulse Framework v1

## Overview

The Institutional Pulse Framework is designed to estimate the evolving condition of organizational life within higher education institutions.

The framework interprets organizational interactions as dynamic governance signals.

Rather than focusing on isolated incidents, the framework evaluates:
- trust conditions,
- participation quality,
- innovation climate,
- adaptive capacity,
- and institutional resilience.

---

# Core Pulse Dimensions

## 1. Trust Pulse

Measures:
- institutional trust,
- relational legitimacy,
- communication confidence,
- social cohesion.

### Positive Indicators
- trust mediation
- collaborative dialogue
- reconciliation
- participatory openness

### Negative Indicators
- communication gaps
- distrust
- policy opacity
- conflict escalation

---

## 2. Participation Pulse

Measures:
- inclusiveness,
- governance participation,
- deliberative engagement,
- institutional voice.

### Positive Indicators
- collaborative decision-making
- open discussion
- faculty engagement
- student representation

### Negative Indicators
- exclusion
- procedural domination
- symbolic participation
- disengagement

---

## 3. Innovation Pulse

Measures:
- adaptive learning,
- institutional experimentation,
- openness to change,
- innovation support.

### Positive Indicators
- experimentation
- digital innovation
- collaborative learning
- adaptive governance

### Negative Indicators
- procedural inertia
- risk avoidance
- innovation blockage
- bureaucratic rigidity

---

# Pulse Interpretation Categories

| Pulse Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| +4 to +5 | Strong Positive Institutional Energy |
| +1 to +3 | Healthy Adaptive Condition |
| 0 | Neutral / Transitional |
| -1 to -3 | Organizational Strain |
| -4 to -5 | Critical Institutional Tension |

---

# Analytical Orientation

Pulse analysis should:
- identify emerging institutional risks,
- recognize adaptive recovery signals,
- interpret governance legitimacy,
- detect organizational fatigue,
- support institutional reflection.

Pulse analysis is NOT intended for:
- individual punishment,
- surveillance,
- political manipulation,
- authoritarian governance.

---

# Longitudinal Interpretation

Institutional pulse should be interpreted over time.

Key considerations:
- recurring emotional patterns,
- repeated governance tensions,
- trust recovery trajectories,
- participation shifts,
- innovation adaptation capacity.

Institutional health is dynamic rather than static.

---

# Reflective Governance Principle

The framework supports:
- human-centered governance,
- adaptive institutional learning,
- participatory legitimacy,
- sustainable organizational development.

The objective is organizational understanding, not institutional control.
